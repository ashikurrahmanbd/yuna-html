ABOUT THE PROJECTS

CareFirst BlueCross BlueShield is a health insurance provider serving 3.5 million individuals and groups in Maryland and the Washington metropolitan area.

I was brought in as the Lead UX Designer to help build the UX team and promote UX value across the organization. The goal was to build a unified experience across BCBS platforms and 13 lines of businesses.

TIMELINE 3 years

MY ROLE

  • Led a core team of 11 designers
  • Led the research and design work across 13 lines of businesses
  • Acted as a voice to influence the value of UX across organization

COLLABORATORSVP, Product, Engineer, Communication

Initial Problem

BlueCross BlueShield has more than 13 lines of businesses with more than 50 platforms. We have observed inconsistencies across these platforms that create gaps in the user experience.

UX Strategy

I created UX strategy and divided the UX activities/deliverables into two parallel tracks. The strategy was planned to align CareFirst FEPOC's brand identity with the desired user experience at every user touchpoint. Based on the UX Strategy, I closely worked with the product teams on product roadmaps to keep usability a top priority at every stage of development.

UX Kanban and Design Sprints

I used lightweight concepting and quick wireframes to kick off the process, and kept the deliverables light and editable. The goal was to get the core components of the idea or workflow visualized quickly and in front of the team and encourage early feedback. We kept the investment at each phase lean and minimal that rethinking, reconfiguring or redesigning wouldn't crush in workload.

UX Maturity, Process and Wiki

I led the effort of using the UX Maturity Model as a framework to assess UX-related strengths and weaknesses of CareFirst's digital products. We used that assessment to determine which of the 4 stages our organization currently occupies and how to advance to the next stage, and ultimately the Customer Experience level. I also took the lead to develop the enterprise UX Process and UX Wiki site. UX strategy was planned based on the Maturity Model and Process.

Design System

I led the UX team to develop a Design System with a set of standards and UI components to manage design at scale by reducing redundancy while creating a shared language and visual consistency across different pages and channels.

UX Education

I was one of the primary planners and presenters of UX monthly workshops to provide education to the entire organization. It also helped to increase UX team's visibility and embed UX into the Agile process.

Discovery

Heuristic Review and Competitive Analysis

Prior to starting work on a new project, I always conducted Heuristic Review on the UI against Nielson Norman's usability principles. The analysis resulted in a list of potential usability issues ranked by UX scores and severity levels. Competitive Analysis was also included to provide strategic insights into the features, functions, flows, and feelings evoked by the design solutions of competitors.

I presented the results with both qualitative and quantitative findings to the stakeholders and project teams.

Contextual Observation with UX Metrics

I led the effort of User Interviews and Focus Groups both in person and remotely. Sometimes, we observed how participants perform their primary tasks while having them describe what they are doing through their interaction with our product in their natural habitat and context of use. I encouraged the team to use Rainbow Spreadsheet so we were able to collaboratively observe UX research sessions and record detail findings.

UX Metrics such as success, time and error were also carefully captured to provide the quantitative data.

Persona and Journey Map

I led the UX and project teams to conduct interviews and data analysis to ensure that Persona and Journey Maps are derived from research with real users. We created persona for each CareFirst's primary user group, and journey map for each primary task. The user journeys we created start before users even engage with our products, and continue after they leave.

At the end, we superimposed the key journeys onto the existing sitemap diagram to look for gaps between various channels and to understand where the experience falls through.

Key pain points

After careful user research and interviews, I discovered the following pain points

Inconsistency of UX/UI across platforms

Lack of collaborations across departments

No personalized experience to target individuals with relevant content and interface

Challenges in making everything accessible and compatible with 508

Lack of mobile device support

Ideation beyond imagination

I planned a led a remote future journey mapping session where 48 steakholders got togetner to come up with the idea of Self Service Portals in order to addreess the pain points derived from complex systems.

SELF SERVICE PORTALS

Provide end to end experience

Personalize experience with AI

Allow customization

Free up service employees to take on more complicated issues

Be accessible and responsive

Future User Flow and Service BluePrint

Based on the research findings, I created the To-Be User Flows to layout the path between AI and human interventions , which ensure that end users and enter and exit any pages smoothly. At the end, we merged all user flows and collaboratively worked with the product team to create To-Be Service Blueprint to visualize the backstage processes across different platforms and user groups. It also helped to create a shared understanding in cross-functional teams.

Sitemap and Information Architecture

Based on the results of Tree-Testing/Card-Sorting, To-Be User Flows, and Service BluePrints, we created Sitemaps with a hierarchical diagram of the applications that shows how pages are prioritized, linked, and labeled.

PROTOTYPING AND TESTING

Post iteration, I worked towards creating an app named Experiaa! which intended to service the gift-giving process. Planned as an assistant for users, the app developed from a combination of the previously mentioned features and an outcome of a series of user testings.

I used paper wireframes to conduct user testings, a few insights that came out of these 5 people user testing were -

  1. The initial design had multiple features that made the app complex and difficult to understand for the user- for the next prototype I focused on designing an MVP for the first phase which had the potential to develop into a business in the future.
  2. The home screen lacked a search button which made it difficult for the users to look for specific products.
  3. The users wanted to get notifications regarding upcoming events as birthdays and anniversaries that could help them plan gifts in advance.
